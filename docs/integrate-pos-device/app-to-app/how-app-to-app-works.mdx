App to App is two apps working together on one VPOS Media device: your Android
app and Nayax's payment app. Your app runs the screens and decides when to take
a payment, and Nayax's app runs the payment and reports back what happened.

The two apps talk through the VPOS Service API, a Java library you add to your
app.

## Why App to App

Building payments into a device app is normally slow and expensive. You take on
the PCI and EMV burden, you integrate separately with each acquirer, and you
often add extra hardware just to give the app a display.

App to App removes that work. Nayax provides a ready-to-use Android SDK and
keeps the payment processing and compliance. Unlike bare-metal terminal SDKs
that stop at card capture, Nayax processes the transaction end to end and
returns the result to your app.

## How the Two Apps Talk

The connection uses AIDL, the standard Android mechanism that lets one app call
methods in another. You never touch card data or payment code: your app binds to
Nayax's payment service (Nayax calls it the VPOS service), then sends requests
and receives results. Nayax keeps full control of the card reader, EMV
processing, PIN entry, and the connection to its backend.

At a high level, every integration follows the same shape:

1. Connect to the VPOS service and wait until it reports that it is ready.
2. Register your app through the `general` class.
3. Call a payment method when a consumer wants to pay.
4. React to the results Nayax sends back through listener callbacks.

## The Parts You Use

The VPOS Service API is large, but a payment integration needs only three parts.
The rest of the library is available in the SDK reference.

* **The remote connector** (`vpos_service_remote_connector`) opens and manages
  the connection to the VPOS service, and tells you when it is ready.
* **The `general` class** registers your app and returns device information,
  plus platform features such as centralized logging and remote parameters.
* **The `payment` class** runs the payment itself, from card detection through
  authorization and settlement.

## How Results Come Back

The VPOS Service API is asynchronous. You make a call, and instead of waiting for
it to finish, you receive the results later through callbacks. You implement
listener interfaces, and Nayax calls them as the payment progresses.

You work with two listeners:

* `device_listener_i` reports on the device. Its `on_ready` callback signals
  that the payment APIs are ready to call.
* `payment_listener_i` reports the steps of a payment.

The payment flow pages show which `payment_listener_i` callbacks fire and in what
order, and the SDK reference lists every callback.

## Built-In or Custom Payment UI

During a payment, the screens (present card, processing, result) have to come
from somewhere. You choose with the `showUI` boolean, the last argument on every
payment call, and the choice is per call.

* **Built-in UI (`showUI = true`):** Nayax draws the payment screens on top of
  your app. This is the fastest path to go live, and Nayax owns the screen while
  the payment runs.
* **Custom UI (`showUI = false`):** your app draws every screen, using the
  `payment_listener_i` callbacks to know what to show. Nayax still handles the
  card, EMV, and PIN securely. This gives you the most control and suits
  branded, multi-step experiences.
