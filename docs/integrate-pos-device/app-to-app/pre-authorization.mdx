A pre-authorization payment splits into two phases with a pause in between. You
place a hold on the card, deliver the product or service, and only then capture
the amount the consumer actually owes.

Use pre-authorization when the final amount is not known until after the service
is delivered, such as a metered wash or a variable-weight purchase. When the
amount is fixed before payment, use [pre-selection](/docs/integrate-pos-device/app-to-app/pre-selection)
instead.

## Authorize, Then Settle

The flow uses two `payment` calls. `payment.authorize` places the hold, and
`payment.settle` captures the final amount against the transaction the
authorization returned.

You start with `authorize`, passing the amount, the channels to accept,
`pre_auth` set to `true`, a `payment_listener_i`, and a `showUI` flag. When the
hold is approved, `on_authorization` returns a `transaction_info_t` that carries
the transaction ID you settle against later.

```java
payment.authorize(
    1000,                         // amount to hold, in minor units (10.00)
    payment.CHANNEL_CONTACTLESS,  // channel bitmask
    true,                         // pre_auth
    new payment_listener_i() {
        @Override
        public void on_authorization(boolean authorized, transaction_info_t info) {
            if (authorized) {
                long transactionId = info.transaction_id;
                // Deliver the product, then settle this transaction.
            }
        }
        // on_card_detected, on_settlement, and on_cancel omitted for brevity.
    },
    true);                        // showUI: Nayax draws the payment screens
```

After you know the final amount, call `payment.settle` with the transaction ID,
the amount to capture, optional consumer information for a digital receipt, the
listener, and the `showUI` flag. Pass `null` for the consumer information when
you do not need it.

```java
payment.settle(
    transactionId,   // from on_authorization
    800,             // final amount to capture (8.00), can be <= the hold
    null,            // optional transaction_consumer_info_t for the receipt
    paymentListener,
    true);           // showUI
```

<Note>
The settled amount can be less than or equal to the authorized amount, so you
can hold a maximum, deliver less, and capture only what the consumer owes.
</Note>

## Cancel a Transaction

To release a hold or stop an in-progress transaction, call `payment.cancel`
with the transaction ID, a reason code, a `payment_listener_i`, and the `showUI`
flag. The service reports the result through `on_cancel`.

```java
payment.cancel(transactionId, payment.CANCEL_TYPE_CANCELLED_BY_CONSUMER, paymentListener, true);
```

## Flow Breakdown

The pre-authorization flow runs in two phases, with your app delivering the
product between them. The service reports each step through the listener.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Your App Starts the Authorization">
    The consumer selects a product or service in your app. Your app calls
    `payment.authorize` with the amount to hold, the channels to accept,
    `pre_auth` set to `true`, and a `payment_listener_i`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Service Waits for a Card and Authorizes">
    The service powers up the reader, calls `on_card_detected` when the consumer
    presents a card, runs the authorization, and calls `on_authorization`. An
    approved result carries the transaction ID.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your App Delivers the Product">
    With the hold approved, your app runs the machine, opens the door, or
    delivers the service, and determines the final amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your App Settles the Final Amount">
    Your app calls `payment.settle` with the transaction ID and the final
    amount. The service captures the funds and calls `on_settlement`.
  </Step>
</Steps>
