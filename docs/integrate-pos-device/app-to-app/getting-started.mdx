Getting started has two parts: the access and tools you get from Nayax, and the
build steps you follow on a test device. Together they take you from your first
request for access to your first payment.

## Prerequisites

Before you write any code, get these from Nayax:

* **Partner access:** contact your Nayax customer success manager or Professional
  Services team and sign the integration agreement.
* **The SDK:** download the `vpos-api` SDK (the AAR), the API reference, and the
  sample app from DevZone.
* **A test device:** Nayax ships a VPOS Media 4 or VPOS Media 4 Mini in test mode
  for development.

<Note>
The runnable sample application is provided by Nayax Professional Services as
part of SDK onboarding. Ask your Nayax contact for the latest sample app and
APK.
</Note>

## Build Your Integration

Once you have access, the SDK, and a test device, work through these steps. Each
one links to the guide with the details.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the SDK to Your App">
    Add the `vpos-api` AAR and the required manifest entries to your Android
    project (see [Connect to the VPOS Service](/docs/integrate-pos-device/app-to-app/connect-to-the-vpos-service)),
    then install your APK on the test device alongside Nayax's payment app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect and Register">
    Bind to the VPOS service, register your application, and wait for the ready
    callback. See [Connect to the VPOS Service](/docs/integrate-pos-device/app-to-app/connect-to-the-vpos-service).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a Payment">
    Call a payment method and handle the result callbacks. Start with
    [Pre-Selection Payment](/docs/integrate-pos-device/app-to-app/pre-selection),
    or use [Pre-Authorization Payment](/docs/integrate-pos-device/app-to-app/pre-authorization)
    when the final amount is not known up front.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Certify and Go Live">
    Run the certification suite with Nayax Professional Services, then deploy
    your APK alongside the production payment app and roll it out to your fleet.
  </Step>
</Steps>
